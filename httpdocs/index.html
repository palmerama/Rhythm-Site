No instructions

You know that feeling when you see a toy and you just have to pick it up and play with it? That’s what our stuff’s like: you want to play with it and you don’t need to be told how.

Get lost *

The more comfortable and in-control you are, the more you’ll try new things. The design of our interfaces allows users to explore without the fear of getting lost.

* don’t get lost.

Bash my buttons

A Game lives or dies on the design of its interface. Over the years we’ve learnt loads from people playing games we’ve created. We apply these ideas to keep users engaged, whatever the content.

Quick and clever

Knowledge of the latest technology and years of design and development experience mean we just get stuff done.

The Rhythm Section

Rhythm is Ed & Adam. We're two interactive designers who see design and code as part of the same creative process. We met at a falconry display in the West Midlands where Adam was in the clutches of a terrible hawk. Ed's freakish height allowed him to reach over the crowds and pluck the owl man from the bird's evil tallons. Thus, a great partnership was born.


Between us, we’ve created a shed-load of award-winning user experiences for exhibitions, museums, websites and mobile platforms which have been enjoyed all over the world.


Former clients include big names such as Google, The BBC, Sony PS3, The National Football Museum, Diesel, Coca-Cola, Kellogg’s, Santander, E*Trade, Microsoft, Umbro, Bentley, The Science Museum, Orange, American Express, Channel 4, National Express and Cadbury.


Adam has a beard (see above), but is not actually some kind of strange owl man thing (see above).


Ed likes small horses, preferably with shoes on, and seeing for large distances across open ground.
